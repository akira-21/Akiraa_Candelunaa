Attack on Titan Trivia

Trivia 1

The design of the Titans was inspired by Hajime Isayama's experience with an inebriated customer at the internet cafe where he worked. The man’s inability to communicate and irrationality inspired the eerie, unpredictable nature of the Titans.

Trivia 2

Eren's name is derived from the Turkish word "Eren," meaning "saint" or "holy person." Ironically, this contrasts with his often aggressive and chaotic behavior in the series.

Trivia 3

Levi Ackerman's age was a topic of speculation for years until Hajime Isayama confirmed that he is in his early 30s. Despite this, his youthful appearance and skills make him one of the most feared soldiers.

Trivia 4

The three walls — Maria, Rose, and Sina — are named after real people in Hajime Isayama’s life. These names are a subtle homage to friends and family members of the creator.

Trivia 5

Hajime Isayama once considered canceling Attack on Titan due to challenges with the storyline, but he persevered, and the series became a global success.

Trivia 6

Attack on Titan was originally serialized in Bessatsu Shōnen Magazine, where it gained a massive following and eventually led to an anime adaptation that premiered in 2013.

Trivia 7

The series explores complex themes like freedom, survival, and the moral implications of war, making it much more than just a story about giant monsters.

Trivia 8

Hajime Isayama, the creator, has stated that he had many ideas for the ending throughout the series, but he only finalized it after much consideration and input from fans.

Trivia 9

The ODM gear, used by the characters to fight Titans, was inspired by various real-world technologies, blending creativity with practicality.
